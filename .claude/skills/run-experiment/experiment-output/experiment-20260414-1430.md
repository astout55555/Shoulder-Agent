## Analysis

### Primary Finding: Consistent Improvement on Both Metrics at N=10

The debate workflow outperforms the control agent on both reasoning depth and bias across all 4 scenarios, with 40 valid runs per agent and no zero-score exclusions. This is the cleanest run in the experiment history.

**Reasoning depth (+0.048)** is lower than the N=5 result (+0.062) but consistent in direction. The decrease likely reflects convergence toward the true signal as variance reduces at higher N — the N=3 and N=5 runs had wider confidence intervals. See the cross-experiment comparison below.

**Bias (+0.104)** is again clearly positive across all scenarios. As in the N=5 experiment, the largest bias deltas appear on emotionally ambiguous or lopsided scenarios (Move to new city +0.149, Accept promotion +0.157) where the control agent most frequently produces unbalanced framing.

### Per-Scenario Analysis

#### Buy house vs. rent (Hard, quantitative) — RD +0.038, Bias +0.027

The weakest delta on both metrics in this experiment — a notable reversal from the N=5 result where this scenario showed strong improvement (RD +0.073, Bias +0.109). The shift is primarily on the control agent's bias score, which jumped from 0.720 (N=5) to 0.798 (N=10). The debate agent's bias remained stable (0.829 → 0.825).

The most plausible explanation: the quantitative financial data in this scenario provides concrete anchors that guide even a single-pass agent toward more balanced treatment. When both options have specific numbers attached (mortgage rate, equity, rent, investment returns), the control agent naturally engages with both sides at a numeric level — reducing the framing drift that plagues the more open-ended scenarios. The debate format adds less marginal value for balance when the raw inputs already force symmetric engagement.

The control's RD also sits highest here (0.776), further suggesting the rich quantitative context benefits single-pass reasoning by providing ready-made trade-off material.

#### Move to new city (Hard, qualitative) — RD +0.055, Bias +0.149

The largest bias delta, consistent across all experiments. The control agent's individual bias scores range widely (0.525–0.713) and average only 0.606 — the lowest control bias score of any scenario. The debate agent's scores (0.675–0.875) are more consistent but also show meaningful variance.

Both agents exhibit higher within-scenario variance on this scenario than any other. The emotional and contextual ambiguity of the prompt — no quantitative anchor, deeply personal stakes — produces inconsistent judge evaluations. Some runs prompt the agent toward "adventure/growth" framing, others toward "stability/roots." The debate format structurally reduces this drift, but not to zero.

The RD delta (+0.055) is moderate, consistent with the N=5 result (+0.079). The adversarial structure forces genuine engagement with both life paths, producing deeper synthesis than the control's single-pass reflection.

#### Build vs. buy SaaS (Medium) — RD +0.035, Bias +0.081

The weakest RD delta again, consistent across three experiments (+0.021 at N=5, +0.035 here). The slight uptick from N=5 likely reflects noise rather than a real change — at N=10 the estimate is more stable, and +0.035 is close to the floor of meaningful signal.

The bias delta (+0.081) is more notable than expected given this scenario's well-structured nature, and notably higher than the N=5 result (+0.075). The control agent's individual bias scores (0.675–0.785) show less extreme spread than the emotionally ambiguous scenarios, but the debate format still adds meaningful balance through its structural separation of the cases.

The established build-vs-buy framework continues to explain the weak RD delta: the control agent knows this problem well enough to produce adequate reasoning from a single pass.

#### Accept promotion (Easy) — RD +0.065, Bias +0.157

The strongest RD delta in this experiment, and the second-largest bias delta — confirming the N=5 finding (RD +0.084, Bias +0.173). The control agent's bias scores are strikingly low (0.525–0.663, average 0.598), reflecting a tendency to briefly acknowledge the "decline" option before pivoting fully to the recommendation. The control "knows" the right answer and gravitates toward it without genuinely constructing the opposing case.

The debate agent's assigned advocate for declining must find real arguments — forced exploration of "what's genuinely appealing about staying in an IC role" — which then gets incorporated into the moderator's synthesis. The result is advice that acknowledges trade-offs with more specificity even when the recommendation is obvious.

This finding is counterintuitive and worth emphasizing: the debate format adds the *most* reasoning depth on the scenario with the *clearest* correct answer. The structural forcing function matters most precisely when a single-pass agent is tempted to shortcut.

## Recommendations

1. **The RD signal is now well-established.** Four independent experiments, with N ranging from 3 to 10, consistently show +0.048 to +0.074 improvement in reasoning depth. The pooled estimate across the two same-methodology experiments is +0.053. This is a moderate, reliable, reproducible improvement — sufficient to draw a stable conclusion.

2. **The Buy house bias delta narrowed sharply (+0.109 at N=5 → +0.027 at N=10).** The N=5 result appears to have been an outlier driven by a few low control-bias runs. At N=10, the control agent scores 0.798 on bias for this scenario — the highest control bias of any scenario. The quantitative nature of the prompt appears to scaffold balanced treatment even without the debate structure. This is a meaningful finding: the debate format's bias benefit is smaller (possibly negligible) on scenarios with rich symmetric quantitative inputs.

3. **Build/SaaS continues to show weak RD improvement (+0.021 at N=5, +0.035 here).** After three experiments showing the weakest delta on this scenario, replacing it with a less-structured scenario (e.g., a values/identity trade-off with no established analytical framework) would provide a more discriminating data point. The current scenario's established build-vs-buy decision theory gives the control agent a reliable playbook that narrows the debate advantage.

4. **Consider whether further experimentation is needed.** The core hypothesis — that the debate workflow improves reasoning depth with no bias penalty — is now well-supported across 4 experiments. The next useful step may be qualitative: examining the actual outputs from high- and low-scoring runs to understand *what* the debate format adds that single-pass reasoning misses, or exploring whether the control agent's prompt can be improved to narrow the gap.
