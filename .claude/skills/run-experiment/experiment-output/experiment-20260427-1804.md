# Experiment: 2026-04-27 18:04 (N=10, Qwen agents + gpt-5-mini judges)

**Config:** 4 scenarios × 10 runs × 2 agents = 40 debate + 40 control scored outputs.
**Zero-score exclusions:** none. All 80 scored outputs are valid.
**Agent configuration:** **`qwen/qwen3.5-2b` via LM Studio** at `http://localhost:1234/v1` for the 4 workflow agents (debate-a, debate-b, control, moderator). **`openai/gpt-5-mini` for the 2 LLM-judge scorers** (reasoning-depth, advice-bias).

This is the first full-N experiment in the cross-model generalizability series. **Not pooled with prior gpt-5-mini-agents experiments** — the agent model is the experimental variable. Scores are not directly comparable to those experiments in absolute terms (the judge is now grading qwen-2b output against the same calibration anchors used for gpt-5-mini output, which lands lower on the scale).

**Other config notes:**
- `itemTimeout: 600s` (Qwen-2B is slower per call than gpt-5-mini).
- `maxConcurrency: 1` (Qwen agent calls go to local LM Studio; concurrent requests caused context-exceeded errors during smoke-test).
- `defaultOptions.modelSettings.maxOutputTokens = 2048` on every agent.
- 6 transient `STRUCTURED_OUTPUT_SCHEMA_VALIDATION_FAILED` errors during the run (qwen-2b sometimes drops a field from the moderator/control structured output). All were absorbed by Mastra's `maxRetries: 3` — every final scored row is valid.

## Interpretation

### Headline

Pooled across all 4 scenarios, the debate workflow beats Qwen's single-pass control on both metrics: **RD Δ = +0.080**, **Bias Δ = +0.128**. Both deltas are larger than the post-optimization gpt-5-mini steady-state (+0.026 RD, +0.076 Bias). Three of four scenarios are positive on RD, three of four are positive on Bias.

### Per-scenario picture

The signal is concentrated on two scenarios:

- **Buy a house now** (hard, quantitative): RD +0.203, Bias +0.262 — the largest deltas in the table. Qwen's single-pass control on this scenario scored very low (RD 0.221, Bias 0.128), which I read as the control struggling to handle the rich quantitative inputs (mortgage rates, equity calculations, ROI comparisons). The debate format scaffolds a more complete analysis because each side advocates for one option's numbers. **This is the scenario that showed the *smallest* effect on gpt-5-mini.** Inverted ordering — interesting and worth noting.
- **Accept promotion** (easy): RD +0.073, Bias +0.290. Bias delta is the largest on this scenario, mirroring the gpt-5-mini pattern. The control's bias score (0.226) is very low — likely the qwen control just declares "take the promotion, it's obvious" without genuinely engaging with the case for declining. The debate forces an advocate for declining and the bias score recovers (0.516).

The other two:

- **Move to new city** (hard, qualitative): debate edges control on RD (+0.093) but **loses on bias by 0.055**. This is unusual relative to gpt-5-mini, where Move to new city was the strongest bias improvement. Looking at the debate's bias variance (range 0.21–0.78 across 10 runs vs control's tight 0.60–0.74), one debate run dropped to 0.212 — likely a moderator output that strongly favored one side. With N=10, that one outlier moves the mean ~0.04. The signed delta is small enough that more N would be needed to know if this is real or sampling noise.
- **Build vs SaaS** (medium): RD goes negative (−0.051) — the only RD-negative cell. Bias is essentially flat (+0.014). The qwen control happens to do well on this scenario (RD 0.593, the highest control RD across scenarios) — possibly because the build/buy framework is well-represented in qwen-2b's training data. The debate format introduced one severely low debate RD outlier (0.250) which pulled the debate mean down; without it, debate RD would be ~0.575, just above control. Again N=10 boundary territory.

### Reading vs gpt-5-mini

Cross-model headline comparison:

| | gpt-5-mini agents | Qwen agents | Direction |
|---|---|---|---|
| Pooled RD Δ | +0.026 | **+0.080** | ~3× larger on Qwen |
| Pooled Bias Δ | +0.076 | **+0.128** | ~1.7× larger on Qwen |
| Bonferroni-passing cells (RD) | 2/4 | tbd | — |
| Bonferroni-passing cells (Bias) | 4/4 | tbd | — |

The directional pattern that the debate workflow's advantage is **larger** on a weaker model is consistent with the architectural-benefit hypothesis: when the single-pass agent struggles more (because the model is less capable), the structural support of debate buys more headroom. The Bias improvement persists strongly on Qwen, replicating the gpt-5-mini finding. RD improvement looks larger on Qwen than on gpt-5-mini, but the per-cell pattern is different — Qwen's biggest RD improvement is on the quantitative scenario (where gpt-5-mini's was the smallest). I don't want to over-interpret this without significance testing.

### Variance and judge behavior

The gpt-5-mini judge produces a much more discriminating distribution than the Qwen judge did in Run 1 of the test pool. RD spans [0.221, 0.690] across cells; Bias spans [0.128, 0.659]. Per-run variance is also higher than gpt-5-mini-on-gpt-5-mini (e.g. debate Move-to-city Bias range 0.21–0.78 over 10 runs), reflecting that Qwen's outputs vary more than gpt-5-mini's.

### Notable outliers worth flagging

A few debate runs scored very low and pull the means down:
- Buy house run 5 debate RD = 0.287 (others cluster 0.4–0.55)
- Build vs SaaS run 5 debate RD = 0.250 (others 0.46–0.65)
- Accept promotion run 5 debate RD = 0.325 (others 0.47–0.63)

Run 5 (or whichever experiment was numbered 5) seems to have produced lower-quality debate output across multiple scenarios. Could be a single bad random seed; could be a structured-output retry that returned a degraded response. These aren't being filtered (they're not zero), which is correct, but worth noting that the noise floor on a 2B-parameter model is higher than on gpt-5-mini.

### Recommendations

1. **Pipeline is healthy and the experiment is interpretable.** Proceed to statistical analysis on this single N=10 dataset (N=40 per group pooled, N=10 per cell).
2. **Don't pool with the gpt-5-mini-agent experiments** — agent model is the variable.
3. **Consider whether to do additional N**. The two scenarios where the per-cell delta is small/negative (Move-to-city Bias, Build/SaaS RD) might separate cleanly with N=20. But for the question "does the debate advantage generalize to a weaker model?", the current N=10 pooled data is already informative.
4. **No methodology changes indicated.** The structured-output retries handled the qwen quirks; no zero-score exclusions; full 40/40 valid runs each side.
